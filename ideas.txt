Second Opinion
===============

Tagline: Your report, decoded. Your visit, prepared.

---

Track: Health and Wellbeing
Hackathon: Anthropic x UMD Hackathon 2026

---

The Problem
-----------

Every day, millions of people receive medical reports they cannot read, from a
system that does not explain itself. They guess. They Google. They either panic
and rush to the ER, or dismiss something serious and wait too long.

This is especially true for students, uninsured individuals, and anyone without
a doctor they can call. Medical knowledge is locked behind jargon. Most people
navigate it alone.

The Solution
------------

Second Opinion is an AI-powered patient preparation tool. Upload your lab
results, blood panel, or radiology report. Three AI specialists analyze it
simultaneously from different angles:

  - The Optimist     finds what is normal, stable, and reassuring
  - The Skeptic      flags values that deserve follow-up or clarification
  - The Systems      Thinker connects findings to each other and to the
                     bigger picture

A fourth synthesis agent reconciles their debate into:

  - A plain-English summary (no jargon)
  - A ranked list of questions to ask your doctor
  - A triage stamp: Reassuring / Watchful / See Doctor Soon
  - Nearby clinics tuned to urgency level (ER, urgent care, or primary care)

No diagnosis. No jargon. No guessing.
Just you, walking into your next appointment knowing exactly what to say.

---

What Makes It Different
-----------------------

Most health tools either diagnose (legally risky, often wrong) or remind you to
take pills (useful but simple). Second Opinion occupies the gap between them.

It does not tell you what is wrong. It tells you what to ask.

The three-agent architecture means different failure modes get caught by
different lenses. The Skeptic might flag a borderline creatinine value. The
Systems Thinker connects it to an elevated WBC. The Optimist notes that
everything else looks stable. A single AI call would give you one perspective.
This gives you three that disagree with each other — which is closer to how
real clinical review actually works.

One sentence: Not a second doctor. A first advocate.

---

How It Works
------------

1. User uploads a medical report (PDF, image, or paste text)
2. Three Claude agents run in parallel via Promise.all()
3. Each returns structured JSON: findings, concerns, strengths, questions
4. Synthesis agent reconciles all three into a final checklist
5. Nearby clinics render based on urgency tone

Tech stack:
  - React 18
  - Anthropic Claude Sonnet (claude-sonnet-4-20250514)
  - Streaming API for live token display
  - pdfjs-dist for PDF text extraction
  - Claude Vision API for image/photo uploads
  - No external UI libraries

---

Challenges
----------

  - Keeping all three agents from returning overlapping findings
  - Designing prompts that prevent any agent from speculating on diagnosis
  - Streaming three simultaneous responses cleanly into separate UI cards
  - Balancing medical usefulness with responsible safety framing

Disclaimer
----------

Second Opinion is a preparation tool only. It does not provide medical
diagnoses, treatment recommendations, or replace professional medical advice.
Always discuss your results with a qualified healthcare provider. In an
emergency, call 911.
